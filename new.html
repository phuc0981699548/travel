Phuket

Phuket (Thai: ภูเก็ต, pronounced phu-ket; Malay: Talang or Tanjung Salang) is one of the southern provinces of Thailand. It includes Phuket Island, the country's largest island, and 32 other smaller islands off the coast. It is located off the west coast of Thailand on the Andaman Sea. Phuket Island is connected by Sarasin Bridge to Phang Nga Province in the north. The nearest province is Krabi, to the east across Phang Nga Bay.

Hawaii

Hawaii (pronounced "Ha-oai"; Hawaiian: Hawaiʻi) is an American state located entirely on the Hawaii Islands (formerly known as the Sandwich Islands by Europeans), located in the Pacific Ocean off the mainland about 3,700 kilometers (2,300 miles) away. When the state joined the Union on August 21, 1959, Hawaiʻi became the 50th state of the United States. The name of the state in Hawaii has the accent ʻokina (ʻ), so Standard Hawaii English (Hawaii dialect) must also be written with it. It is the state with the highest percentage of Asian residents in the United States.

California

California (pronounced "California" or "California", if fast: "California"), is also called briefly by the Vietnamese. Cali, is a state on the west coast of the United States. With a population of 38 million[1] and an area of ​​410,000 square kilometers (158,402 mi2), California is the most populous state in the United States and the third largest by area.
